üèÄ The History of Basketball

Basketball began with its invention in 1891 in Springfield, Massachusetts, by Canadian physical education instructor James Naismith as a less injury-prone sport than football. Naismith was a 31-year-old graduate student when he created the indoor sport to keep athletes active during the winters. The game quickly gained popularity, first in America and then worldwide. The NBA, established in 1946, grew to a multibillion-dollar enterprise by the end of the century, making basketball an integral part of global culture.

Did you know?
A game similar to basketball is mentioned in a 1591 book published in Frankfurt am Main, describing a skill game where balls were thrown at a target woven from twigs, mounted high on a pole. The modern game was created by Dr. James Naismith in December 1891, using peach baskets and a soccer ball. The first public basketball game was played on March 11, 1892.

üìã Standard Basketball Protocols

  • Winning the game: Team with the most points at the end of regulation (or overtime) wins.
  • Substitutions: Unlimited, but only during stoppages or timeouts.
  • Fouls:
    • Personal foul: Illegal physical contact
    • Team fouls: Accumulated per quarter/half; leads to bonus free throws
    • Technical foul: Unsportsmanlike behavior or delay of game
    • Disqualification: After 6 fouls (NBA) or 5 fouls (FIBA/NCAA)
  • Violations:
    • Traveling: Moving illegally with the ball
    • Double dribble: Dribbling, stopping, then dribbling again
    • Shot clock violation: Not shooting before the shot clock expires
    • Backcourt violation: Taking the ball back over midcourt after crossing
  • Shot Clock Duration: 24 seconds
  • Scoring:
    • Field goal inside arc: 2 points
    • Three-point shot (beyond arc): 3 points
    • Free throw: 1 point
